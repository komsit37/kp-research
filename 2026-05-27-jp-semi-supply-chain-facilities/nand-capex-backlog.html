The NAND cycle is digesting. Multiple JP filers explicitly postponed NAND-related capex through FY25–FY26: SUMCO paused its Yoshinogawa wafer plant, KOKUSAI Electric flagged a "temporary slowdown in NAND tool investment" (recovery only at FY3/2028), and Lasertec's mask-blank weakness was pinned directly to Samsung NAND/foundry capex revisions. Order-book confirms it: NAND-tooling + power-semi + auto-semi backlogs are all down −13% to −37% YoY, while only the HBM/AI-substrate names (Ibiden +109%, JEM +44%, Micronics +19%) show rising backlogs. The lone bullish NAND read is TEL, which cited "NAND investment +42.5% in 2025" on its May-2026 call — explained by its broader logic+memory etcher mix. The upstream lever for the whole chain is Kioxia's own Yokkaichi/Kitakami fab spend.
