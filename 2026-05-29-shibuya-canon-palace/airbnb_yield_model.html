What's deliberately not in here (worth knowing)

These omissions make NOI slightly optimistic — fold them in before underwriting.
  • Capex / FF&E reserve — furniture, appliances and refurb wear out far faster under STR turnover. A reserve of ~3–5% of revenue is normal; it's not included here. At the 50% high-season case that's ~¥5M/yr, which would pull the net yield from 4.14% to ~3.88%.
  • Income tax and financing costs — model is unlevered / pre-tax.
  • Property-tax reassessment on change of use, and higher building management from heavier common-area turnover (trash, wear) — both kept flat/conservative.
  • Ramp / vacancy drag — assumes the target occupancy is hit from year one; a new listing typically takes 6–12 months to reach steady-state reviews and pricing.
  • Airbnb platform fee — is captured, but as the explicit ×0.84 line above opex, not inside the 38% variable rate.
⚠️ Legal constraint (decisive). Canon Palace is zoned Residential. minpaku (住宅宿泊事業法) is allowed but capped at 180 nights/yr (~49% occupancy); 旅館業 (hotel licence) is year-round but generally not permitted in residential zones. The two 50% columns are the practical legal maximum; the 70% column needs a licence path that residential zoning likely blocks.
Modelling notes. Unlevered/all-cash.
