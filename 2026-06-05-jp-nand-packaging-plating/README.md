## Headline findings

1. **The "mixed" NAND comments aren't noise — two capex vectors decoupled.** Wafer/greenfield **capacity** capex (SUMCO, Shin-Etsu) is being deliberately withheld; **technology-upgrade** capex (200→300-layer + bonding — TEL, KOKUSAI, Disco, Tri Chemical) is accelerating; a third **HBM/AI-packaging** vector (Advantest, Ibiden, the plating chain) runs hot and independent. 2026 NAND WFE is +34% YoY but "process upgrades, not capacity."

2. **It's a supply crunch, not a demand air-pocket** — NAND wafer prices +~246% since early-2025; makers hold capacity into a price spike (discipline + HBM crowd-out). Real lead-lag: wafer (SUMCO) leads the tool-makers down by ~4–6 quarters.

3. **The investable edge is advanced-packaging plating chemistry.** JCU (Cu via-fill ~70%) and Uyemura (ENEPIG, sole-qualified) own *different* sequential steps on the *same* line — not competitors. Both scale with AI-substrate area + layer count + panel-level packaging. JCU = higher-beta, Ibiden-concentrated, China-levered (~47.5%); Uyemura = wider moat, clean Taiwan/Japan/Korea geo (~6.5% China), Atotech PLP-ENEPIG the main threat.

4. **JCU's Kumamoto plant ≠ a TSMC JASM play** — JASM is a front-end logic fab that doesn't consume packaging chemistry; the linkage is a stock narrative. The real JCU/Uyemura leading indicator is **substrate-maker capex** (Ibiden ¥500B FY26-28; Unimicron NT$34B in 2026), not Kumamoto wafer output.

5. **The ABF 2nd-order materials crunch (Nittobo T-glass ~90%, Mitsui Kinzoku MicroThin foil ~95%) is largely real but a 2–3yr supply-squeeze, not a 5yr compounder** — glass-core is the post-2029 tail risk (hits Nittobo harder than Ajinomoto), and there is no clean listed pure-play (AGC is the least-bad proxy; all the chokepoint names are conglomerate-diluted).
