  1. The "mixed" NAND comments aren't noise — two capex vectors decoupled. A capacity (wafer/greenfield — SUMCO, Shin-Etsu) is deliberately withheld; B tech-upgrade (200→300-layer + bonding — TEL, KOKUSAI, Disco, Tri Chem) is accelerating; C HBM/AI packaging (Advantest, Ibiden, the plating chain) runs hot and independent.
  2. It's a supply crunch, not a demand air-pocket. NAND wafer prices +~246% since early-'25; Kioxia 2026 sold out. Makers hold wafer capacity (discipline + HBM crowd-out) even into a price spike — which is exactly why capacity names stay soft while tech-upgrade names inflect.
  3. Real lead-lag, quantified: wafer (SUMCO) leads the tool-makers down by ~4-6 quarters; consumables/probe-cards lead the capacity-tool names.
  4. The investable edge is in advanced-packaging plating chemistry — JCU (4975, Cu via-fill) and C. Uyemura (4966, ENEPIG) — driven by AI-substrate area + layer growth + panel-level packaging. Their real leading indicator is substrate-maker capex (Ibiden, Unimicron), NOT TSMC's Kumamoto wafer fab.
  5. The ABF 2nd-order materials crunch (Nittobo T-glass, Mitsui Kinzoku foil) is largely real but a 2-3yr supply-squeeze trade, not a 5yr compounder — glass-core is the post-2029 tail risk, and there's no clean listed pure-play (AGC is the least-bad proxy).

5 · ★ Advanced packaging → plating demand (JCU / Uyemura)

The most investable angle. JCU and Uyemura own DIFFERENT plating steps on the SAME line — they are not competitors. JCU = the electroplated-Cu-volume play (via-fill + RDL); Uyemura = the surface-finish + seed play (ENEPIG + electroless Cu). Both ~40% / 23% OP-margin chemistry franchises.

JCU (4975) owns

  • Cu via-fill electroplating ~70% (CU-BRITE) — the core moat
  • SAP/mSAP RDL pattern plating ≤2µm (TIPHARES RDP) — the growth engine (Kumamoto)
  • TGV/TSV Cu fill — natural extension

Uyemura (4966) owns

  • ENEPIG surface finish (Ni/Pd/Au) — sole-qualified, no JEDEC Pd-free alternative as of Jan'26
  • Electroless Cu seed (organic RDL, PLP)
  • Cu pillar / wafer-level UBM (EPITHAS)

Myth-bust — JCU Kumamoto ≠ TSMC JASM play. JCU's ¥11.4B Kumamoto plant is near JASM (~15km) but the linkage is a stock narrative, not operational. JASM is a front-end logic fab (consumes Cu-damascene ECP from MacDermid/Atotech, not JCU); JCU makes back-end packaging chemistry for OSATs/substrate makers. JCU never names TSMC in its filings (cites BCP + Kyushu cluster). FY3/26 proof: JCU's Taiwan revenue rose sharply (AI substrate at Unimicron/Ibiden) while JASM Fab 1 idled at ~50%. TIPHARES revenue target is only ~¥1B FY3/27 — a long-dated option on a future Japan packaging cluster, not a near-term JASM beneficiary.

Glass-core counter — weaker on 3yr, real on 5yr; hits Nittobo harder than Ajinomoto. Glass-core eliminates T-glass cloth + CCL core + core-foil, but RETAINS ABF film for RDL build-up (so Ajinomoto partially survives). Intel halted internal glass R&D Aug'25 → licensing; TSMC CoPoS volume only 2028-29; glass yield ~75-85% vs organic >95%. Net: ~10% haircut on 3yr, ~20-30% on 5yr.
Are NVIDIA & AMD both eliminating T-glass? No. AMD's move is a glass interposer (sits inside the organic package — doesn't touch T-glass). NVIDIA is evaluation-only (Rubin ships on organic ABF). T-glass elimination needs glass-core (2028-29, ASIC-led via Samsung SEMCO→Broadcom). Meanwhile T-glass demand rises ~3×: Hopper ~3,190mm² → Rubin ~8,000mm² package area + the ASIC ramp.
