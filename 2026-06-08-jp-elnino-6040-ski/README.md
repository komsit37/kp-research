# Nippon Ski Resort Development (6040) — Does El Niño Actually Hurt the Business?

**Date:** 2026-06-08

**Question tested:** A common bear framing — *"the coming El Niño means a warm, low-snow winter, which should be bearish for a ski-resort operator like 6040, on top of weaker inbound Chinese tourism."* Is that chain real, with data?

**Data sources:** IRI/NOAA ENSO forecasts (May 2026); JMA-relationship climate literature & ski-season reports; J-Quants financials; TDnet tanshin — Q3 FY2026 (doc 1191665, released 2026-06-05) and history.

---

## Verdict

| Thesis component | Status | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| El Niño is coming this winter | ✅ TRUE | IRI 19 May 2026: **97-98% El Niño DJF 2026-27**, La Niña ~0%; Niño3.4 already +0.9°C |
| El Niño → warmer / lower-snow Japan | ✅ TRUE (directional) | 2023-24 El Niño = Japan's 2nd-hottest winter (+1.27°C DJF); 70+ resorts closed temporarily, 15 never opened |
| Less snow → fewer 6040 skiers | ❌ REFUTED by company data | Winter 2025-26 (poor snow): visitors **1,880k, −0.3%**, inbound **543k record +23.3%** |
| Less snow → lower 6040 earnings | ❌ REFUTED by natural experiment | FY2024 (strong-El-Niño winter): sales **+19.5%**, OP **+50%** — a record |
| Weak Chinese inbound hurts 6040 | ⚠️ MISDIRECTED | Hakuba foreign mix is majority Australian; China is a Niseko/urban-retail story |
| **Net** | **Thesis weak on stated grounds** | El Niño = likely **sentiment dip**, not a fundamental break. Real risk is margins. |

---

## 1. The premise holds — but it's the only part that does

- **ENSO:** IRI (19 May 2026) and NOAA CPC both put **El Niño at 97-98%** through DJF 2026-27, La Niña effectively 0%. The Pacific is already transitioning (Niño3.4 +0.9 °C).
- **Climate link:** El Niño warms Japan and weakens the Siberian cold-air / Sea-of-Japan snow machine. The last strong El Niño (2023-24) produced Japan's **2nd-hottest winter on record** (+1.27 °C DJF); **70+ resorts closed temporarily and 15 never opened**. Central Honshu (Nagano/Niigata — where most of 6040 sits) had ~50% odds of below-average snow.

Both true. The thesis fails at the next link.

## 2. The natural experiment: 6040 grew *through* the worst El Niño on record

The 2023-24 strong-El-Niño winter falls inside 6040's **FY2024** (FY ends 31 July):

| Fiscal year (Aug→Jul) | Winter ENSO | Sales | YoY | Op profit | YoY |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| FY2023 (Aug22–Jul23) | La Niña | ¥6.90bn | — | ¥1.04bn | — |
| **FY2024 (Aug23–Jul24)** | **Strong El Niño** | **¥8.25bn** | **+19.5%** | **¥1.55bn** | **+50%** |
| FY2025 (Aug24–Jul25) | La Niña (weak) | ¥10.46bn | +27% | ¥2.25bn | +45% |
| FY2026E | **El Niño (fcst)** | ¥11.48bn | +9.7% | ¥2.30bn | +2.4% |

In the worst El Niño winter on record, sales rose +19.5% and operating profit +50%. Structural drivers (inbound, snowmaking, green-season, M&A) swamped the weather.

## 3. Winter 2025-26 confirms demand is weather-resilient (hard counts)

The Q3 FY2026 tanshin (released 2026-06-05, covers Aug 2025–Apr 2026) describes a **poor-snow La Niña winter**: opened on snowmaking + late-Nov natural snow, then temps rose and **full-course opening slipped to mid-January**; **February onward had no natural snow** — snowmaking carried the season. Despite that:

- **Total winter visitors 1,880k, −0.3% YoY** — flat vs the all-time-record 24-25 season (1,886k).
- **Inbound visitors 543k, +23.3% YoY — a new record** (vs 440k), led by Hakuba.
- By resort, the **snowmaking-invested resorts grew**: Tsugaike +11.2%, Iwatake +5.2%, Kawaba +9.5%; only Happo-one fell (−12.1%).
- NSD Kids season-pass users 93k (−0.6%) vs a prior *heavy-snow* season — flat.

ENSO phase is a weak predictor of *actual* Japan snow (a La Niña winter delivered poor natural snow), and even poor snow did not dent demand because snowmaking + inbound dominate. **The snow→demand link is empirically broken for this name.**

### Why 6040 is structurally insulated
- **Snowmaking is the strategy** — 11 new machines at Tsugaike (Loop 4000), ¥100m snowmaking/gates at Kawaba. When 70+ resorts closed in 2023-24, 6040's kept running.
- **Geography** — Hakuba Valley (~half of revenue) sits in one of Japan's heaviest-snow zones; mild winters still ski. El Niño hits *low-elevation* resorts hardest (for 6040 the minority: Kawaba, Meiho, Zao Eboshi).
- **Diversification** — green season ramping (~256k summer visitors at Iwatake in 2025); revenue ~70% winter and falling as a share.
- **Inbound** — booked months ahead, weather-tolerant; the structural growth engine.

## 4. The "weak Chinese inbound" leg misfires for 6040

Hakuba's foreign visitors are **majority Australian** (industry mix ≈ 30% AUS / 20% US / 15% SE Asia; mainland Chinese a minor, emerging slice). The China wave is a **Niseko / urban-shopping** story. 6040's real inbound sensitivities are **AUD/JPY, the Australian consumer, and global ski-travel demand** — not China. (Caveat: 6040 runs a Chinese-language inbound consulting arm and China is growing, so not literally zero.)

## 5. The real pressure has nothing to do with El Niño: margins + one-offs

The Q3 FY2026 P&L (9M, Aug25–Apr26):

| Line | FY2025 9M | FY2026 9M | YoY |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sales | ¥9,159m | ¥9,977m | **+8.9%** |
| COGS | ¥4,518m | ¥5,180m | **+14.6%** |
| Gross profit | ¥4,641m | ¥4,797m | +3.4% (GM 50.7%→48.1%) |
| SG&A | ¥1,794m | ¥2,078m | **+15.8%** |
| **Operating profit** | ¥2,847m | ¥2,719m | **−4.5%** |
| Recurring profit | ¥2,839m | ¥2,736m | −3.7% |
| Gain on sale of fixed assets | ¥3m | **¥1,102m** | (Iwatake land) |
| Impairment loss | ¥6m | ¥112m | — |
| Net profit (parent) | ¥1,911m | ¥2,229m | +16.6% |

- **Operating deleverage:** sales +8.9% but OP −4.5%. COGS +14.6% and SG&A +15.8% outran revenue — labour, snowmaking energy, and rising depreciation from heavy capex (construction-in-progress jumped ¥754m→¥2,657m; machinery ¥2,829m→¥3,008m).
- **Profit is propped by a one-off:** the +16.6% net-profit growth — and the full-year forecast where **NP ¥2,470m > OP ¥2,300m** — is the **¥1.1bn gain on selling land at the foot of Iwatake Resort** (footnoted explicitly; disclosed 2024-09-30). Underwrite on *operating* profit, not headline net. Forecast P/E ~8.7x is flattered by this gain.
- **Balance sheet leverage rising:** long-term borrowings ¥2,663m→¥4,413m to fund capex; equity ratio 61.2%→55.4%.
- Note the El Niño *cost* channel: a warm winter raises snowmaking energy spend — so El Niño's most plausible 6040 impact is **margin**, not units. That pressure is already visible above, in a non-El-Niño year.

## 6. Implications

- "El Niño damages 6040 fundamentals" is weak. "El Niño headlines may produce a sentiment dip in a structurally weather-resilient operator" is the more accurate read.
- After the 2023-24 record, the market may have already learned 6040 is weather-resilient, so any weather-driven dip may be shallow.
- What actually matters more than ENSO: (1) margin compression / operating deleverage; (2) profit dependence on asset sales (the Iwatake gain won't recur); (3) AUD/JPY and Australian ski-travel demand; (4) rising leverage funding capex.
- Snapshot (5 Jun 2026): price ¥465, mcap ¥21.4bn, TSE Growth. FY2026E sales ¥11.48bn / OP ¥2.30bn / NP ¥2.47bn (one-off-inflated).

## Limitations

The financial history is short and confounded (COVID recovery + post-2022 inbound boom + snowmaking capex + M&A all at once), so a clean snowfall elasticity can't be isolated from the P&L. But the confound runs *against* the thesis — even in the dirty data the El Niño year was a standout, and the company's own visitor counts show demand holding through poor snow.
